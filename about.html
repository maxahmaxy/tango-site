Our Teachers

Masters of the Embrace

Our instructors carry decades of experience from the milongas of Buenos Aires. They don't just teach steps — they transmit a way of feeling.

Portrait
Buenos Aires, Argentina 25 years dancing • 18 years teaching

Marcelo Ríos

Founder & Lead Instructor

Marcelo discovered tango at fifteen in the legendary milongas of San Telmo. By twenty, he was already teaching in the cultural centers of La Boca, learning from the old milongueros who remembered tango's golden age.

His teaching philosophy centers on the embrace as the foundation of all movement. "Without connection," he says, "there is no tango — only choreography."

Teaching Philosophy

"Tango is patience. It cannot be hurried. Every step must be earned through feeling, not memorization."

Portrait
Córdoba, Argentina 22 years dancing • 15 years teaching

Valentina Aguirre

Co-Director & Senior Instructor

Valentina's journey began with classical ballet in Córdoba before she fell irrevocably in love with tango during a summer in Buenos Aires.

Known for her precise technique and deep understanding of the follower's role, Valentina has dedicated herself to empowering dancers to find their own voice within the embrace.

Teaching Philosophy

"The follower is not passive. She is the heart of the dance — interpreting, embellishing, and completing the conversation."

Portrait
Montevideo, Uruguay 20 years dancing • 12 years teaching

Diego Fernández

Instructor & Music Director

Diego brings a musician's soul to his teaching. Trained as a bandoneonist before becoming a dancer, he understands tango from the inside out.

His classes are renowned for their deep dive into musicality. Students learn to distinguish between orchestras and to dance to the breath of the music itself.

Teaching Philosophy

"When you truly hear the music, your body knows what to do. The orchestra is the third dancer in every embrace."

Our Story

Born from a Shared Dream

Alma del Tango was founded in 2008 by Marcelo and Valentina after years of dancing together in the milongas of Buenos Aires. They shared a vision: to create a space where tango could be learned as a living, breathing art form.

What began as small classes in a borrowed studio has grown into a community of passionate dancers from all walks of life. Our mission remains unchanged — to preserve the soul of Argentine tango.

Our Values

What We Believe

Authenticity

We teach tango as it was meant to be danced — with respect for its origins and emotional depth.

Patience

True mastery cannot be rushed. We celebrate every small victory on the journey of learning.

Community

Tango is a social dance. We foster connection and friendship among all who share the floor.

Joy

Above all, tango should bring happiness. We seek the smile that comes from true connection.

We welcome dancers of all levels. Whether this is your first step or your thousandth, there is always more to discover.
